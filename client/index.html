Advanced Infill Model (CLT)
Uses Classical Laminate Theory to compute effective stiffness from bead properties + ply angles — replacing the empirical pattern multipliers with a physically derived model. Stiffness now scales correctly with infill density and deposition angle.
Ply angle stacks by pattern:
Grid → [0°, 90°] equal share  ·  Lines → [0°] only  ·  Gyroid/Cubic/Honeycomb → [0°, 60°, −60°] quasi-isotropic  ·  Concentric → [0°]
Validation targets (from Ahn et al. 2002):
100% infill, 0° only → should recover single-bead E ≈ 3700 MPa (PLA)
±45° at 100% → lower E, higher G than 0° only
E* vs density (10–100%) should match Ahn Fig. 5 within 15%
Note: CLT uses bead-level E₁, E₂, G₁₂ from literature defaults. For best accuracy, calibrate these by printing 0° and 90° dog-bone tensile coupons and entering the results above.
Dog-bone tensile coupon STL
Dog-bone tensile coupon — measures in-plane yield strength and stiffness.
Dimensions: 10mm wide × 4mm thick × 50mm gauge length
Print: flat orientation, 100% infill, same layer height as your target settings
Test: clamp both tabs, pull slowly until fracture. Record peak load.
If you don't have a deflection measurement, leave blank — only yield strength will be calibrated.
Lap shear coupon STL
Lap shear coupon — measures inter-layer bond strength (Z-direction).
Print two identical tabs (20mm wide × 4mm thick × 40mm long). Bond them at their ends with a 20×20mm overlap — the overlap IS the layer interface being tested. Actually this means printing them together as one piece: a flat plate 20×40×4mm with a middle notch.
Print: flat orientation, 100% infill
Test: pull tabs apart along their length. Record peak load.
This directly measures your printer's inter-layer bond strength — the most important unknown in the model.
Bearing coupon STL
Bearing coupon — measures hole wall bearing strength.
Dimensions: 40mm long × 20mm wide × 4mm thick. M3 clearance hole (3.2mm) centered 10mm from one short edge.
Print: flat orientation, your target infill and wall settings
Test: put an M3 bolt through the hole, apply lateral force to the bolt while holding the plate. Record when hole begins to elongate.
This is the only way to get real bearing strength data — literature has none for FDM. Raises bearing failure from LOW to MEDIUM confidence.
FEA stress-concentration correction (Kt)
By default, lap-shear and bearing strengths use nominal F/A stress. Enabling this runs FEA on the coupon geometry to compute the true stress concentration factor Kt, then corrects the derived strength upward — making calibration consistent with how STORMFEA evaluates real parts.
Method limitation (≈5% noise floor): The FEA coupon uses fully-fixed grip nodes, which block Poisson contraction and introduce a small stress artefact near the gripped end. Even a perfectly uniform reference bar returns Kt ≈ 1.05 rather than 1.00. Treat Kt differences below ~10% as noise — only concentrations comfortably above the floor (typically 1.3–2.5× for real lap-shear and bearing geometries) carry meaning.
